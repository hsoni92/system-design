# How to Think as a Senior Consultant

A short guide to frame your mind from a **Senior Consultant** standpoint—aligned with the Microsoft Senior Consultant (A2 – Apps) role and your experience. Use this to switch into consultant mode before interviews or customer conversations.

---

## 1. Senior Consultant Mindset

- **Advisor + architect + hands-on engineer**: You move between “What should we do?”, “How should we build it?”, and “I’ll build it.” You are not only coding; you are shaping strategy, design, and delivery.
- **Outcome-first**: Solutions are judged by **business impact** and **measurable value**, not only by technical correctness. Ask: “What does the customer gain?”
- **Customer- and stakeholder-aligned**: Technical choices are explained in terms of the customer’s **objectives**, **risks**, and **constraints**—not only in tech jargon.

---

## 2. Mental Models (Use These Before You Answer)

| When | Ask yourself |
|------|-------------------------------|
| **Before answering** | What is the **business outcome**? What are the **constraints** (time, budget, compliance, skills)? |
| **When designing** | Is it **scalable**, **secure**, and aligned to **reference architectures** and best practices? Where are the **trade-offs**? |
| **When delivering** | What are the **priorities**, **estimates**, **dependencies**, and **trade-offs**? What could **block success**? |
| **When collaborating** | How does this help **Sales / account teams** articulate value and support **adoption**? |

These tie directly to the role: **technology strategy**, **delivery leadership**, **risk and security/compliance**, **IP reuse**, and **thought leadership**.

---

## 3. Quick Checklists

### Discovery / requirements

- Clarify the **outcome** the customer wants (not just features).
- Identify **users** and **stakeholders**.
- Capture **constraints**: timeline, budget, compliance, skills, existing systems.
- Define **success criteria** (measurable where possible).
- Plan how you’d **validate** understanding with the customer (e.g., prototypes, reviews).

### Solution design

- **Business alignment**: Does the design support the stated objectives?
- **Scalability**: Can it grow with load and data?
- **Security & compliance**: Zero Trust, least privilege, encryption, regulatory requirements.
- **Observability**: Logging, metrics, tracing for production readiness.
- **Reference patterns**: Use known patterns (e.g., RAG, microservices, serverless) where they fit.

### Delivery

- **Scope vs timeline**: Clear priorities; disciplined trade-offs when scope or dates slip.
- **Risks and mitigations**: Identify technical and delivery risks; have mitigation plans.
- **Quality bar**: Definition of done, testing, and release criteria.
- **Release strategy**: Consider Blue/Green, Canary, or phased rollouts where appropriate.

### Stakeholder communication

- **Value proposition** in business terms (e.g., “faster time-to-market”, “lower risk”).
- **Trade-offs and options** presented clearly so stakeholders can decide.
- **When to escalate**: Know what you own vs what needs leadership or customer alignment.

## 5. General KPIs for This Role

How you’d typically be measured as a **Senior Consultant (A2 – Apps)**. Use these to speak to “how I’d be measured” in interviews.

### Delivery

- **On-time, on-budget delivery** of engagements (or within agreed tolerances).
- **Release predictability and quality**: e.g., defect escape rate, production incidents, adherence to release plans.

### Solution quality & alignment

- **Alignment to business objectives** and **Microsoft reference architectures**.
- **Security, compliance, and assurance**: e.g., no critical security findings; adherence to customer, regulatory, and Microsoft governance requirements.

### Customer & growth

- **Customer satisfaction / NPS** or engagement feedback scores.
- **Contribution to expansion and adoption**: e.g., technical value propositions that support follow-on work or broader adoption of Microsoft cloud and AI.

### Leadership & impact

- **Technical risk**: Identification, assessment, mitigation; handling escalations.
- **Reuse and speed**: Use of IP, reusable assets, and best practices to improve **delivery predictability** and **speed**.
- **Thought leadership**: Contributions to continuous improvement, new ways of working, and engineering productivity.
